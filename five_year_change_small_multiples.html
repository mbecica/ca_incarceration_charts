California Justice-Involved Population: 5-Year Change, 1980–2024

Percent change in population over each 5-year period — six programs shown separately for clarity

Sources: CDCR historical records (Zimring & Hawkins compilation); CDCR Monthly Report of Population & Data Points; BJS Annual Parole Survey; CA DOJ OpenJustice; BSCC Jail Profile Survey; LAO; CPOC.
Each panel shows the percent change in December population from 5 years prior, with the area filled to zero (green for growth, red for contraction). All panels share the same x and y axes for direct visual comparison. The 2024 point uses a 4-year window (2020 is the most recent prior anchor).
PRCS and Mandatory Supervision are shown as open dots at 2018 and 2023 only, using two within-source windows (2013→2018 fully CPOC, 2019→2023 fully LAO). The standard 5-year anchors are not used for these programs because they would span methodology changes in the underlying data source — a CPOC published value can't be directly compared to a LAO visualization estimate without surfacing measurement artifacts. The dots aren't connected with a line because the windows aren't sequential 5-year increments.
Major drivers: the 1980s–90s prison surge reflects determinate sentencing reforms; AB 109 (October 2011) drove the 2010–2015 parole and probation contractions; Prop 47 (November 2014) reclassified certain felonies as misdemeanors.
