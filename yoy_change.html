California Justice-Involved Population: Year-over-Year Change, 2011–2024

Annual percent change in population for state prison, county jail, state parole, county probation, PRCS, and mandatory supervision

Sources: CDCR Monthly Report of Population & Data Points; CA DOJ OpenJustice (probation); BSCC Jail Profile Survey; LAO; CPOC.
Each point is the percent change in December population vs the prior year. Y-axis uses a symmetric log scale (linear within ±10%, log-compressed beyond) so the cluster of small year-to-year changes is readable while the AB 109 parole crash and other outliers still fit on the chart. PRCS and Mandatory Supervision lines start at 2014 (using verified 2013 as baseline) — earlier values were Gemini-produced estimates flagged as unverified, so the apparent +95% and +82% jumps in 2012/2013 reflect estimate-vs-reported transitions, not real growth.
PRCS and Mandatory Supervision lines also break at 2018→2019 and 2023→2024 because the underlying data source changes at those boundaries (CPOC published reports → LAO visualizations → Gemini-produced estimates). Computing a year-over-year change across a methodology shift would surface a measurement artifact rather than a real population change, so those points are omitted.
Major inflection points: AB 109 Public Safety Realignment took effect October 2011 (drives the parole −42% drop in 2012). COVID-era prison and jail decarceration in 2020.
