California Justice-Involved Population: 5-Year Change, 1980–2024

Percent change in population over each 5-year period for state prison, county jail, state parole, county probation, PRCS, and mandatory supervision

Sources: CDCR historical records (Zimring & Hawkins compilation); CDCR Monthly Report of Population & Data Points; BJS Annual Parole Survey; CA DOJ OpenJustice; BSCC Jail Profile Survey; LAO; CPOC.
Each point represents the percent change in December population from 5 years prior. Sample years: 1985, 1990, 1995, 2000, 2005, 2010, 2015, 2020, 2024 (the 2024 point uses a 4-year window since 2020 is the most recent prior anchor). Series begin in the first 5-year window with valid data: jail and parole start at 2005 (vs 2000); probation at 2010 (vs 2005).
PRCS and Mandatory Supervision are shown as open dots (no connecting line) at 2018 and 2023, using two within-source windows: 2013→2018 (5-year, all CPOC published reports) and 2019→2023 (4-year, all LAO visualizations). Standard 5-year anchors (2015→2020 and 2020→2024) are not shown for these programs because they would span a methodology change in the underlying data source — comparing values across CPOC, LAO, and Gemini-estimate boundaries surfaces measurement artifacts rather than real population changes.
The 1980s prison surge reflects determinate sentencing reforms and the "tough on crime" era. AB 109 Public Safety Realignment (October 2011) drives the 2010–2015 parole and probation contractions; Prop 47 (November 2014) reclassified certain felonies as misdemeanors.
